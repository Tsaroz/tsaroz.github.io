Seven-Card Stud

A classic poker game where players are dealt a mix of face-up and face-down cards over seven betting rounds. There are no community cards, so reading your opponents' boards is a key skill.

Game Flow

Third Street

Two down cards and one up card are dealt. The player with the lowest up card "brings it in" with a forced bet.

Fourth Street

Each player receives another up card. The player with the highest-ranking hand showing starts the betting.

Fifth & Sixth Streets

Another up card is dealt on each street, followed by a round of betting. The bet size usually doubles on Fifth Street.

Seventh Street (The River)

A final down card is dealt. The last betting round occurs, followed by a showdown.

Key Strategy Concepts

Memory is Your Weapon

Remembering the cards that have been folded is a huge advantage. It tells you which cards are "live" and which are not.

Board Reading

Pay close attention to your opponents' up cards. Their board gives you a lot of information about the possible strength of their hand.

Starting Hand Selection

Trips (three of a kind), big pairs, and three cards to a straight or flush are strong starting hands. Don't play junk!
